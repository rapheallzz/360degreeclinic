IVF Services

Take your first step with 360 Degree Clinic Abuja. Our leading fertility clinic, world-class fertility treatments, and expert care are available to all. Because no matter where you are on your journey to parenthood or how complex your medical needs are, our tireless pursuit of fertility success means we always have treatment options available. That is why our in-depth analysis of your medical history ensures your unique fertility treatment plan will maximize your chance of success.

IVF (In-vitro fertilization)

We use the latest techniques to maximize egg growth, embryo yield, and help you conceive in the shortest possible time.

Natural/mild IVF

If conventional IVF isn’t the best option for whatever reason, we can use lower doses of fertility medication to stimulate your ovaries and egg growth.

IUI (Intrauterine Insemination)

An alternative to IVF involving a simple procedure to place good quality sperm into your womb that’s often used where one partner has a low sperm count.

Follicular tracking

In some circumstances we may recommend monitoring your follicular growth and ovulation during your natural cycle so you can time intercourse just right.

Holistic treatments

Many patients feel the emotional, mental and physical value of supplementary treatments such as Cupping, Nutrition and counseling. These are all supportive treatments.

The IVF process and what to expect

Step 1: A fertility check-up

We put every patient on the best possible treatment plan with the highest chance of success. That process starts with our thorough general and fertility work-up

Step 2: Stimulating your ovaries

To maximize the number of eggs we collect, you will start daily injections of a stimulating hormone over a period of 10-12 days. During this time, we will monitor your response closely with blood tests and ultrasound scans, and optimize your medication doses.

Step 3: Collecting your eggs and sperm sample

We will collect your eggs using a minor procedure normally lasting between 20 to 30 minutes and you will be given a mild intravenous sedation so you do not feel any pain. On the same morning your male partner will produce a semen sample, or frozen or donor sperm can be prepared.

Step 4: Fertilizing your eggs and growing embryos

The prepared eggs and sperm are placed together in a culture dish to allow fertilization to occur. We will therefore monitor the development of the embryos for the next 3 to 5 days. Our embryology team will update you regularly about the development of your embryos.

Step 5: Transfer or Sampling and freezing your embryos

Generally, we transfer good quality embryo on day 5 of fertilization but we mostly recommend genetic testing (PGT-A) to most patients undergoing IVF because this can significantly reduce the risk of implantation failure or miscarriage, and reduce the time and cost of a successful pregnancy. If you are having PGT-A, a highly experienced embryologist will remove a sample (biopsy) of a few cells from each suitable embryo and the samples are sent to the genetics lab for analysis.

Step 4: Frozen embryo transfer

Once the genetics results are back, you will have a consultation with your doctor to discuss the number and quality of the embryos. A plan will be discussed to prepare you for the embryo transfer.

FAQ

Is IVF right for me?

IVF is one of the main techniques we use to help women from all walks of life overcome their fertility issues and have a baby. The process involves stimulating your ovaries and helping them produce as many eggs as possible for collection. Once retrieved, the eggs are fertilized in our state-of-the-art laboratory using a prepared sperm sample to create embryos. Typically, the good quality embryos are transferred into your into your womb to achieve a successful pregnancy.

You might want to use IVF if…

You have unexplained fertility

Your own egg reserve is low

You have a tubal problem

You have endometriosis

Your partner has male factor infertility

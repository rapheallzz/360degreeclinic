LAPAROSCOPIC PROCEDURE

Also called minimally invasive surgery or keyhole surgery, are modern surgical technique. The laparoscope is placed through a small incision made below or inside the navel while other surgical instruments are placed through other incisions, usually 2-3, in the lower abdomen to explore and make a diagnosis or to perform a surgical procedure. This advanced technique is applicable on different gynaecological procedures, such as

- Laparoscopic sterilization

- Pelvic adhesiolysis

- Ovarian cystectomy

- Oophorectomy

- Salpingectomy

- Myomectomy

- Hysterectomy

Advantages of laparoscopy over open surgery are obvious. Patients can benefit from reduced hemorrhaging, less pain, less post-operative scarring and speed recovery. Hospitalization can be shortening to one to two days.

FAQ

Abdominal and pelvic diagnostics

Abdominal and pelvic diagnostics refer to a range of medical tests and procedures used to evaluate the organs and structures within the abdomen and pelvis. These diagnostics may include imaging techniques like ultrasound, CT scans, or MRIs to identify conditions such as tumors, infections, cysts, or organ dysfunction. They help doctors assess reproductive health, digestive issues, and other abdominal or pelvic concerns, providing critical information for diagnosis and treatment planning.

Gallbladder and appendix removal

Gallbladder and appendix removal are common surgical procedures performed to treat conditions affecting these organs. Gallbladder removal (cholecystectomy) is typically done to address gallstones or inflammation, while appendix removal (appendectomy) is performed to treat appendicitis, an infection of the appendix. Both surgeries are usually done through minimally invasive techniques (laparoscopy), resulting in quicker recovery times and reduced risk of complications. These procedures help prevent further health issues and improve the patient's quality of life.

Hernia Repair

Hernia repair is a surgical procedure to correct a hernia, which occurs when an internal organ or tissue pushes through a weak spot in the surrounding muscle or connective tissue. Common types of hernias include inguinal, umbilical, and hiatal hernias. The surgery involves repositioning the protruding tissue and strengthening the affected area, often with mesh for added support. Hernia repair is typically done through minimally invasive techniques, promoting faster recovery and reducing the risk of complications.
